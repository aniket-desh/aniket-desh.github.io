Spring 2024 18 cr
  • University Physics: E&MPHYS 212
    Calculus-based treatment of Coulomb's law, electric fields, Gauss' law, electric potential, capacitance, circuits, magnetic forces and fields, Ampere's law, induction, electromagnetic waves, polarization, and geometrical optics.
    4
  • Relativity & Math MethodsPHYS 225
    Theory of special relativity with applications to kinematics and dynamics, plus key mathematical methods including vector analysis, series expansions, matrices, Fourier analysis, partial differentiation, and simple differential equations.
    2
  • Abstract Linear AlgebraMATH 416
    Rigorous proof-oriented linear algebra covering determinants, vector spaces over fields, linear transformations, inner product spaces, eigenvectors and eigenvalues, Hermitian matrices, and Jordan normal form.
    3
  • Introduction to CS IICS 128
    Continuation of CS 124 with more advanced concepts in computing and techniques for solving computational problems.
    3
  • Discrete MathematicsMAT 206
    Covers sets, relations, functions, basic counting techniques, recurrence relations, graphs and trees, and matrix algebra with an emphasis on algorithms.
    3
  • Nature & American CultureRST 242
    Cultural meanings associated with American natural landscapes, examining colonial, romantic, and conservation perspectives alongside revisionist themes of gender, cultural pluralism, and societal meanings of parks and protected areas.
    3
2024 – 2025   sophomore
Fall 2024 17 cr
  • Data StructuresCS 225
    Data abstractions including elementary data structures (lists, stacks, queues, trees) and their implementation in an object-oriented language, with solutions to computational problems such as graph and tree search and elementary algorithm analysis.
    4
  • Statistics & Probability ISTAT 400
    Introduction to mathematical statistics developing probability as needed, including the calculus of probability, random variables, expectation, distribution functions, central limit theorem, point estimation, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing.
    4
  • Classical Mechanics IPHYS 325
    Kinematics and dynamics of classical systems including a review of Newtonian mechanics, three-dimensional motion, variable mass, conservation laws, damped and driven oscillations, gravitational potentials of extended objects, rotating frames, and Lagrangian and Hamiltonian mechanics.
    3
  • Quantum Info ProcessingECE 498EC
    Basic concepts and principles underlying quantum computing and communication, with equal emphasis on mathematical tools, quantum information processing, quantum communication, and nonlocality and entanglement theory.
    3
  • Global Dev. & EnvironmentGGIS 101
    Geographical perspectives on environment and development with case studies from Africa, Asia, and Latin America, investigating the origins of the global South in relation to the global North and processes driving environmental, economic, and cultural change.
    3
Spring 2025 12 cr
  • Electromagnetic Fields IPHYS 435
    Static electric and magnetic fields, their interactions with charge and current, and their transformation properties under special relativity, including macroscopic fields in material media.
    3
  • Classical Mechanics IIPHYS 326
    Continuation of PHYS 325 covering central force motion, collisions and scattering, rotational motion, coupled oscillations, continuous media, and fluid dynamics.
    3
  • Real VariablesMATH 447
    Careful development of elementary real analysis for students intending to pursue graduate study, covering completeness of the reals, basic topological properties of n-dimensional space, convergence of sequences and series of functions, continuity, differentiation, and Riemann integration.
    3
  • Random ProcessesMATH 466
    Systematic treatment of discrete-time and continuous-time Markov chains and discrete-time martingales, including strong Markov properties, recurrence and transience, invariant distributions, convergence, ergodicity, forward and backward equations, and MCMC techniques.
    3
2025 – 2026   junior
Fall 2025 15 cr
  • Deep Learning TheoryCS 540
    Theoretical foundations of neural networks including constructive and non-constructive approximation with one hidden layer, benefits of depth, optimization in the NTK regime, maximum margin optimization, and generalization bounds via Rademacher complexity, VC dimension, and covering numbers.
    4
  • Neural Dynamical SystemsECE 598RE
    Graduate course exploring the interplay between dynamical systems theory and neural networks (artificial and biological), covering stability, attractors, bifurcations, chaos, Neural ODEs, training dynamics, and neural circuit models including rate-based and spiking networks.
    4
  • Deep Generative ModelsECE 498RCU
    Key advances in generative models including variational autoencoders, normalizing flows, GANs, diffusion models, neural differential equations, flow matching, and Schrödinger bridges.
    3
  • Machine LearningCS 446
    Principles and applications of machine learning covering discriminative and generative methods plus reinforcement learning: linear and logistic regression, SVMs, deep nets, structured methods, dimensionality reduction, k-means, Gaussian mixtures, EM, MDPs, and Q-learning.
    3
  • Information GeometrySTAT 390
    Independent reading on Fischer information, statistical manifolds, neural coding, gradient learning, feature learning, and neural population models.
    1
  • High-Dimensional StatisticsECE 598IS · audit
    Decision theory, information inequalities, Le Cam's and Fano's methods, random matrix theory, spectral estimators, stochastic block models, graph embeddings, Laplacian eigenmaps, Johnson-Lindenstrauss, and PCA.
    0
